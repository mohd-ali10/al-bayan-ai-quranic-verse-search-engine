Innovating Quranic Search

Al-Bayan leverages modern Natural Language Processing to help you explore divine wisdom beyond simple keyword matching.

The Problem & Solution

Traditional digital Quran tools rely on exact keyword matching. If a user searches for "Stress," they might miss verses that mention "anxiety," "fear," or "hardship" simply because the exact word "Stress" isn't in the translation.

Al-Bayan solves this using Semantic Search. By converting verses into vector embeddings, our system understands the context and meaning of your query, retrieving relevant verses even if they don't share a single word with your search term.

The Vision Behind Al-Bayan

Motivation

The primary motivation for this Final Year Project was to bridge the gap between traditional Islamic scholarship and modern Artificial Intelligence. In an era of information overload, finding specific guidance in the Quran requires more than just skimming text‚Äîit requires understanding. Al-Bayan aims to make the Quran more accessible to non-Arabic speakers and researchers by allowing them to search using natural concepts rather than rigid keywords.

Methodology

We moved beyond standard Regular Expressions (Regex) by implementing Cosine Similarity on high-dimensional vector spaces. Using pre-trained BERT-based models (specifically all-MiniLM-L6-v2), we convert every verse into a dense numerical vector. When a user queries, we calculate the geometric distance between the query vector and verse vectors, ranking results by semantic closeness rather than text overlap.

Future Scope

  • Voice Search Integration: Allowing users to recite or speak queries.
  • Multi-lingual Support: Expanding semantic search to Urdu and Arabic directly.
  • Contextual Tafsir Chatbot: An AI assistant to explain verses conversationally.
